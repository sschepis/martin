# **FILM SHOT & TECHNIQUE CHEAT SHEET**

---

## **1. SHOT SIZES (FRAMING)**

### **Extreme Wide Shot (Establishing)**

* **What:** Subject is very small or absent; environment dominates.
* **Use:** Open scenes, define location, show scale.
* **Evokes:** Isolation, grandeur, insignificance, context awareness.

---

### **Wide Shot (Long Shot)**

* **What:** Full body visible within environment.
* **Use:** Introduce characters in space; show movement.
* **Evokes:** Physical presence, spatial grounding, realism.

---

### **Medium Wide (Cowboy Shot)**

* **What:** Frame from mid-thigh up.
* **Use:** Dialogue with body language; action readiness.
* **Evokes:** Balance between character and environment; tension readiness.

---

### **Medium Shot**

* **What:** Waist-up framing.
* **Use:** Standard dialogue coverage.
* **Evokes:** Natural engagement; conversational neutrality.

---

### **Medium Close-Up**

* **What:** Chest/shoulders up.
* **Use:** Emotional dialogue moments.
* **Evokes:** Connection, attention to subtle expression.

---

### **Close-Up**

* **What:** Face fills frame.
* **Use:** Key emotional beats or realization moments.
* **Evokes:** Intimacy, intensity, psychological focus.

---

### **Extreme Close-Up**

* **What:** Isolated detail (eyes, hands, object).
* **Use:** Symbolic emphasis or tension.
* **Evokes:** Obsession, precision, discomfort, heightened awareness.

---

## **2. CAMERA ANGLES**

### **Eye-Level**

* **What:** Camera aligned with subject’s eyes.
* **Use:** Neutral storytelling.
* **Evokes:** Equality, realism, objectivity.

---

### **Low Angle**

* **What:** Camera below subject looking up.
* **Use:** Power dynamics, dominance.
* **Evokes:** Authority, intimidation, awe.

---

### **High Angle**

* **What:** Camera above looking down.
* **Use:** Vulnerability, exposure.
* **Evokes:** Weakness, surveillance, loss of control.

---

### **Bird’s-Eye (Top-Down)**

* **What:** Direct overhead.
* **Use:** Spatial clarity, abstraction.
* **Evokes:** Detachment, fate, system-level perspective.

---

### **Dutch Angle (Tilted Frame)**

* **What:** Frame is slanted.
* **Use:** Instability, disorientation.
* **Evokes:** Psychological tension, unease, imbalance.

---

## **3. CAMERA MOVEMENT**

### **Static Shot**

* **What:** No camera movement.
* **Use:** Let action or dialogue carry scene.
* **Evokes:** Stability, observation, restraint.

---

### **Pan (Horizontal Rotation)**

* **What:** Camera rotates left/right.
* **Use:** Reveal information, follow motion.
* **Evokes:** Discovery, continuity.

---

### **Tilt (Vertical Rotation)**

* **What:** Camera rotates up/down.
* **Use:** Reveal scale or hierarchy.
* **Evokes:** Awe (tilt up), inspection (tilt down).

---

### **Dolly In (Push-In)**

* **What:** Camera moves toward subject.
* **Use:** Emotional emphasis, realization.
* **Evokes:** Intensity, focus, inevitability.

---

### **Dolly Out (Pull-Back)**

* **What:** Camera moves away.
* **Use:** Reveal context or isolation.
* **Evokes:** Separation, loss, detachment.

---

### **Truck (Side Movement)**

* **What:** Camera moves laterally.
* **Use:** Track subjects in motion.
* **Evokes:** Fluidity, continuity.

---

### **Crane / Jib**

* **What:** Vertical sweeping motion.
* **Use:** Large-scale reveals, transitions.
* **Evokes:** Freedom, transcendence, cinematic scope.

---

### **Handheld**

* **What:** Unstabilized camera.
* **Use:** Chaos, realism, urgency.
* **Evokes:** Anxiety, immersion, instability.

---

### **Steadicam**

* **What:** Smooth moving handheld system.
* **Use:** Follow characters fluidly.
* **Evokes:** Immersion, presence, dreamlike flow.

---

### **Zoom (Lens Change)**

* **What:** Adjust focal length without moving camera.
* **Use:** Stylized emphasis.
* **Evokes:** Artificiality, surveillance, sudden focus shift.

---

## **4. LENS & FOCUS**

### **Wide-Angle Lens**

* **What:** Expands field of view; exaggerates depth.
* **Use:** Tight spaces, dynamic motion.
* **Evokes:** Distortion, energy, immersion.

---

### **Telephoto Lens**

* **What:** Compresses distance.
* **Use:** Isolate subjects; flatten space.
* **Evokes:** Intimacy at distance, voyeurism.

---

### **Shallow Depth of Field**

* **What:** Only subject in focus.
* **Use:** Direct attention.
* **Evokes:** Isolation, subjectivity.

---

### **Deep Focus**

* **What:** Everything in focus.
* **Use:** Complex staging.
* **Evokes:** Awareness, realism, layered meaning.

---

### **Rack Focus**

* **What:** Shift focus between planes.
* **Use:** Reveal importance.
* **Evokes:** Realization, shifting attention.

---

## **5. EDITING TECHNIQUES**

### **Straight Cut**

* **What:** Immediate transition.
* **Use:** Standard continuity.
* **Evokes:** Clarity, efficiency.

---

### **Match Cut**

* **What:** Visual or motion continuity between shots.
* **Use:** Symbolic or seamless transitions.
* **Evokes:** Connection, meaning linkage.

---

### **Jump Cut**

* **What:** Time discontinuity within same shot.
* **Use:** Compress time, create unease.
* **Evokes:** Fragmentation, urgency.

---

### **Cross-Cutting**

* **What:** Alternating scenes.
* **Use:** Parallel action.
* **Evokes:** Tension, convergence.

---

### **Fade In / Out**

* **What:** Gradual appearance/disappearance.
* **Use:** Scene boundaries.
* **Evokes:** Passage of time, closure.

---

### **Dissolve**

* **What:** Overlapping transition.
* **Use:** Passage of time or memory.
* **Evokes:** Fluidity, continuity, dream state.

---

## **6. SPECIAL SHOTS**

### **Over-the-Shoulder (OTS)**

* **What:** Shot from behind one subject toward another.
* **Use:** Dialogue scenes.
* **Evokes:** Perspective alignment, relational context.

---

### **POV Shot**

* **What:** Shows what a character sees.
* **Use:** Subjective experience.
* **Evokes:** Identification, immersion.

---

### **Insert Shot**

* **What:** Close-up of object.
* **Use:** Important detail.
* **Evokes:** Significance, clue emphasis.

---

### **Cutaway**

* **What:** Brief alternate shot.
* **Use:** Break continuity or add context.
* **Evokes:** Expansion of scene awareness.

---

### **Tracking Shot**

* **What:** Continuous follow movement.
* **Use:** Action or journey.
* **Evokes:** Momentum, immersion.

---

### **Whip Pan**

* **What:** Fast pan with blur.
* **Use:** Transition or surprise.
* **Evokes:** Energy, disorientation.

---

## **7. LIGHTING**

### **High-Key**

* **What:** Bright, low contrast.
* **Use:** Comedy, commercial, clean visuals.
* **Evokes:** Safety, clarity, optimism.

---

### **Low-Key**

* **What:** Strong shadows, contrast.
* **Use:** Drama, noir.
* **Evokes:** Mystery, tension, depth.

---

### **Backlighting**

* **What:** Light behind subject.
* **Use:** Silhouettes, separation.
* **Evokes:** Mystery, transcendence.

---

### **Practical Lighting**

* **What:** Visible light sources in frame.
* **Use:** Realism.
* **Evokes:** Authenticity, groundedness.

---

## **8. TIME & MOTION MANIPULATION**

### **Slow Motion**

* **Use:** Highlight moment.
* **Evokes:** Gravity, emotional weight.

---

### **Time-Lapse**

* **Use:** Compress long durations.
* **Evokes:** Passage of time, inevitability.

---

### **Long Take**

* **Use:** Continuous action.
* **Evokes:** Immersion, realism, tension.

---

### **Freeze Frame**

* **Use:** Stop motion.
* **Evokes:** Reflection, emphasis.

---

## **HOW TO USE THIS IN PRACTICE**

* **Emotional escalation:** Wide → Medium → Close-Up → Extreme Close-Up
* **Power shift:** Eye-level → Low angle (dominance) or High angle (submission)
* **Tension building:** Static → Slow push-in → Close-Up
* **Disorientation:** Dutch angle + handheld + jump cuts
